Our History

  1. 1878-1882

    The Sonora Railway builds the 1st railroad line in Sonora from Nogales to Guaymas

  2. 1898

    The ATSF swaps the Sonora Railway line for the Southern Pacific California Mojave line , The Sonora Railway is leased by the Southern Pacific Railroad

  3. 1899

    The Phelps Dodge Company incorporates the Ferrocarril de Narcozari a line from Agua Prieta to Narcozari

  4. 1902

    The Cananea Consolidated Copper Company Cananea, incorporates the Rio Yaqui and Pacific Railroad. Southern Pacific soon takes over and builds a line from Naco to Cananea.

  5. 1907

    Brakeman Jesus Garcia saves the town of Narcozari as a train full of TNT for mining catches fire. . Garcia was quick to put his train in reserve full throttle downhill outside of town where a huge explosion occurred killing Jesus Garcia, saving the town of Narcozari.

  6. 1909

    Southern Pacific creates the Sud Pacifico de Mexico (Southern Pacific of Mexico)

  7. 1910-1920

    The Mexican Revolution erupts in Mexico, and delays the SPdeM progress of reaching Guadalajara

  8. 1924

    The SP buys the El Paso & Southwestern Railroad which also included the Narcozari railroad.

  9. 1927

    The SPdeM reaches Guadalajara

  10. 1937-1947

    The Ferrocarril Sonora-Baja California is created by the Mexican Government to connect Mexicali, Baja California to Sonora, worked is delayed due to World War II, the SBC connects the SPdeM line at Benjamin Hill

  11. 1951

    The SP sells to the Mexican Government in December, the Ferrocarril Del Pacifico is created

  12. 1965-1967

    The SP sells the Ferrocarril Narcozari to the Mexican Government becoming part of the FCP system, a Line is built from Naco to reach Agua Prieta

  13. 1987

    All the Railroads of Mexico became part of the FNM system SBC and FCP become part of the Pacifico region

  14. 1994-1998

    The railroads of Mexico become Privatizatized. SBC and FCP assets are sold to Ferromex.
